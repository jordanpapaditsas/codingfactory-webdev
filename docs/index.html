The Princess of Ionian

It is no coincidence that Corfu is known as the "Princess of the Ionian Sea", nor that it is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Greece. The island seems to have everything to offer, from a beautiful country, infinite beaches and streets that "shout" history, to musical culture, special culinary tastes and very hospitable people. Its historic center has been included in the UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Its mountainous northern part is occupied by Pantokrator, on whose slopes Ano Perithia, the oldest settlement of Corfu, is built. At the other end of the island is the unique lake of Korission, one of the most important wetlands of the island, with the lagoon and sand dunes combined with a unique forest of rare cedars found exclusively on this side of the Mediterranean.
